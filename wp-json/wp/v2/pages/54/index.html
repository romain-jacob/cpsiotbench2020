    Data Availability Statement<\/h3>\n

    Public data sharing is strongly encouraged<\/strong> for all submitted papers, but not compulsory. However, all papers must<\/strong> include a statement about data availability. We recommend to put this statement as a paragraph between the abstract and the introduction. The statement should indicate what data is made available by the authors, and where they can be accessed. Minimally, we recommend that the authors publish the processed data used to produce all plots and visualizations presented in the paper<\/strong>.<\/p>\n

    For good advice on data sharing, see The Turing Way<\/a> handbook, most particularly<\/p>\n

      \n
    • Chapter 3.1 – Open Data<\/a><\/li>\n
    • Chapter 7 – Research Data Management<\/a><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      Shall the authors decide not to share any data related to the paper, the statement could simply be the following sentence: \u201cThe authors of this paper do not make any corresponding data available<\/em>.\u201d Again, we do not recommend this…<\/p>\n

      Anonymity<\/h3>\n

      As an author, you are required to make a good-faith<\/strong> effort to preserve the anonymity of your submission. Common sense<\/strong> and careful writing will go a long way towards preserving anonymity. Minimally,<\/p>\n

        \n
      • Remove the names and affiliations of authors from the title page.<\/li>\n
      • Remove acknowledgment of identifying names and funding sources.<\/li>\n
      • Use care in naming your files. Source file names (e.g., \u201cAlice-n-Bob.pdf\u201d) are often embedded in the final output as readily accessible comments.<\/li>\n
      • Do not embed pointers to external sources (e.g., public GitHub repositories) that leak author identity or affiliation.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

        If you find it difficult for your submission to meet these requirements, you may contact the organizers<\/a> for advice.<\/p>\n\t

        Do fill-in<\/strong> the author names and email addresses OpenReview.net when submitting your work. The submission will not display the names.<\/p>\n\t

        Paper presentation<\/h3>\n

        Authors of accepted papers are expected to present their work<\/strong> at the workshop. The timing and format of the presentation will be precised at a later stage.<\/p>\n
